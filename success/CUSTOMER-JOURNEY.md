# Merline Customer Journey

## Journey Overview

The Merline customer journey spans 11 stages from initial awareness through advocacy. Each stage has defined activities, milestones, success criteria, stakeholder engagement, risk factors, team handoffs, and timeline expectations.

---

## Stage 1: Awareness

### Customer Activities
- Encounters Merline through content marketing, conference presence, partner referral, or direct outreach
- Visits website and reads about AI-native MERL platform
- Downloads whitepapers or case studies
- Attends webinar or conference session
- Researches competitive alternatives

### Milestones
- First website visit
- Content download or webinar attendance
- Expresses interest via contact form

### Success Criteria
- Prospect understands Merline's value proposition (AI-native, end-to-end MERL OS)
- Prospect self-identifies as fitting target segment (NGO, consultancy, government, research)

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| M&E Director / Head of Research | High | Targeted content, conference conversations |
| Program Manager | Medium | Blog posts, case studies |
| IT Manager | Low | Technical whitepapers, architecture docs |

### Risk Factors
- Prospect does not understand AI value proposition (over-education required)
- Prospect already deep in procurement with competitor
- Prospect views Merline as "too early stage"

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| Marketing | Sales / Business Development | Lead score threshold met (download + org fit) |

### Timeline
- 1-8 weeks depending on channel (inbound faster than outbound)

---

## Stage 2: Evaluation

### Customer Activities
- Discovery call with sales to assess fit
- Receives tailored demo showing end-to-end workflow
- Evaluates against specific use case and pain points
- Technical evaluation: security questionnaire, compliance requirements
- References call with existing customer (if available)
- Trial period (14-30 days) with onboarding support

### Milestones
- Discovery call completed
- Tailored demo delivered
- Trial account created
- Trial first submission received
- Technical evaluation completed

### Success Criteria
- Prospect confirms Merline addresses their core MERL workflow gaps
- Technical evaluator clears security and integration requirements
- At least 3-5 users from prospect have hands-on trial experience
- Time-to-value demonstrated: first data collected on platform within trial

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| M&E Director (Champion) | Very High | Discovery, demo, trial check-ins |
| IT Manager (Evaluator) | High | Security review, integration discussion |
| Program Manager (User) | Medium | Demo, trial participation |
| CFO / Budget Owner | Low-Medium | Pricing discussion, ROI calculator |
| Procurement / Legal | Low | Later in process (Stage 4) |

### Risk Factors
- Prospect cannot articulate specific use case
- Security requirements exceed Merline's current compliance posture (SOC 2 deferred to Phase 2)
- Competitor already deployed and embedded
- Trial stalls: users don't engage within first 7 days
- Prospect expects AI features not yet available (Phase 2)

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| Sales / BD | Sales + Solutions Engineer | Deal qualified (BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) |
| Sales | Customer Success (early engagement) | Trial activated |

### Timeline
- Small NGO / Consultancy: 2-4 weeks
- Mid-size NGO: 4-8 weeks
- Large NGO / Government: 8-16 weeks

---

## Stage 3: Procurement

### Customer Activities
- Receives pricing proposal
- Negotiates terms, discounts, and contract length
- Legal review: DPA, SLA, Terms of Service
- Security review: completes questionnaire or receives SOC 2/GDPR documentation
- Purchase order processed or payment submitted

### Milestones
- Pricing proposal accepted
- Contract signed
- Payment received / PO issued

### Success Criteria
- Contract signed within proposed terms (±10% negotiation)
- Annual commitment preferred (improves retention)
- Implementation scope documented

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Budget Owner / CFO | High | Pricing, ROI, contract negotiation |
| Legal / Procurement | High | Contract, DPA, SLA review |
| Champion (M&E Director) | Medium | Internal justification support |
| Customer Success (incoming) | Low | Introduction before kickoff |

### Risk Factors
- Legal review stalls on standard terms (DPA, data residency, liability caps)
- Budget cycle misalignment (funds not available until next fiscal)
- Competitor makes last-minute discount offer
- Champion leaves organization during procurement
- Government procurement adds 3-6 months of RFP process

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| Sales | Customer Success | Contract signed, payment received |
| Sales | Implementation Team | Contract signed, onboarding scheduled |

### Timeline
- 1-4 weeks (NGO/consultancy) to 4-12 weeks (government/large org)

---

## Stage 4: Planning

### Customer Activities
- Kickoff meeting with Merline Customer Success and customer project team
- Define implementation scope, timeline, and success criteria
- Identify customer project manager and stakeholder team
- Inventory current tools, data, and workflows
- Map migration path: what moves first, what moves later
- Establish communication plan and governance structure
- Schedule training sessions

### Milestones
- Kickoff meeting completed
- Implementation plan signed off
- Success criteria documented and agreed
- Communication plan established
- Training schedule confirmed

### Success Criteria
- Customer has clear understanding of what will be implemented, in what order, by when
- Customer project manager identified and empowered
- Success criteria are specific, measurable, and aligned to business outcomes
- Both parties agree on go-live definition

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Customer Project Manager | Very High | Weekly planning calls |
| Executive Sponsor | Medium | Kickoff attendance, milestone reviews |
| IT/Admin | High | Technical setup planning |
| Researchers/M&E | Medium | Training needs assessment |
| Field Supervisors | Low | Input on field readiness |

### Risk Factors
- Customer has not identified a dedicated project manager
- Unrealistic timeline expectations (expecting full deployment in 1 week)
- Stakeholder resistance not yet surfaced
- Scope creep from customer (wanting all features immediately)
- Data migration complexity underestimated

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| Implementation Team | Customer Success Manager | Implementation plan finalized |
| Sales (handoff complete) | Customer Success (full ownership) | Kickoff completed |

### Timeline
- 1-2 weeks

---

## Stage 5: Implementation

### Customer Activities
- Organization setup (branding, timezone, security policies)
- User provisioning and role assignment
- Data migration from existing tools (CSV/Excel import)
- Integration setup (API, DHIS2, etc. — Phase 2+)
- Configuration of indicator library
- Design of first questionnaire(s)
- Setup of dashboard templates and report templates
- Mobile app deployment to enumerator devices

### Milestones
- Organization configured
- Users imported with correct roles
- First study created and configured
- First questionnaire designed and approved
- Mobile app installed on enumerator devices
- Data migration completed (if applicable)

### Success Criteria
- Customer admin can independently manage users and permissions
- Indicator library reflects customer's measurement framework
- First questionnaire is production-ready (reviewed, approved)
- Enumerators can log in to mobile app and see assignments

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Customer Admin/IT | Very High | Weekly implementation sessions |
| Lead Researcher/M&E Officer | High | Content design guidance |
| Customer Project Manager | High | Weekly status calls |
| Executive Sponsor | Low-Medium | Milestone email updates |

### Risk Factors
- Data quality issues discovered during migration
- User resistance: researchers don't want to leave existing tools
- Mobile app compatibility issues with device fleet
- Localization needs (language, offline maps) not yet addressed
- Customer underestimates time needed for questionnaire design

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| Implementation Lead | Customer Success Manager | Setup complete, training begins |
| Customer Success | Training Lead | Training schedule confirmed |

### Timeline
- 2-4 weeks depending on complexity

---

## Stage 6: Training

### Customer Activities
- Admin training (organization management, user administration)
- Researcher/M&E training (study design, questionnaire builder, dashboards)
- Supervisor training (assignment management, data quality monitoring)
- Enumerator training (mobile app, offline workflow, sync)
- Manager/Executive training (dashboards, reports)
- Hands-on practice with sandbox environment
- Knowledge assessment and certification

### Milestones
- Admin training completed
- Researcher training completed
- Enumerator training completed
- Manager training completed
- Training completion rate > 80% per role

### Success Criteria
- Admins can independently manage users, roles, and settings
- Researchers can create studies, design questionnaires, and generate reports
- Enumerators can download forms, collect data offline, and sync
- Supervisors can monitor team progress and quality flags
- Managers can view dashboards and generate reports

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Researchers/M&E Officers | Very High | Hands-on workshops |
| Enumerators | High | Field-based mobile training |
| Supervisors | High | Dashboard training |
| Managers/Executives | Medium | Executive briefing |
| Admin/IT | High | System administration training |

### Risk Factors
- Enumerator low digital literacy requiring extended training
- Training not completed before go-live date
- Staff turnover: trained users leave before go-live
- Language barriers: training materials not available in local language
- Insufficient practice time between training and go-live

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| Training Lead | Customer Success Manager | Training completion report |
| Customer Success | Support Team | Go-live imminent |

### Timeline
- 1-2 weeks (can overlap with Implementation)

---

## Stage 7: Go-Live

### Customer Activities
- Final go-lice checklist review
- Production environment validation
- First real data collection on platform
- Data sync verification
- Dashboard and report validation
- Go-live sign-off from customer project manager

### Milestones
- Go-live checklist 100% complete
- First real-world submission received and synced
- Dashboard showing live data
- Customer project manager signs off
- Support channels confirmed and tested

### Success Criteria (Go-Live Gates)
- All P0 workflows operational: study → form → assignment → collection → sync → dashboard
- No critical or high-severity defects open
- Customer can collect, sync, and view data independently
- Support channels established and tested
- Customer confirms readiness

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Customer Project Manager | Very High | Go-live verification |
| Executive Sponsor | High | Go-live notification and demo |
| All End Users | High | Go-live communication |
| Merline Support | High | Monitoring readiness |
| Customer IT | High | Production validation |

### Risk Factors
- Last-minute data migration issues
- Mobile app store approval delays (if required)
- Network connectivity issues in field
- User confidence low despite training
- Skeptical executive who needs convincing with real data

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| Implementation + Training | Customer Success (full ownership) | Go-live sign-off |
| Customer Success | Support (ongoing) | Go-live confirmed |

### Timeline
- Day 1 of real data collection. Go-live is a moment, not a period.

---

## Stage 8: Optimization

### Customer Activities
- Post-launch support: first week of full production
- 7-day health check with Customer Success Manager
- Workflow refinement based on real usage
- Additional feature adoption (new modules, advanced features)
- Data quality review and improvement
- User feedback collection and prioritization
- Process documentation and best practice capture

### Milestones
- 7-day health check completed (green)
- 30-day health check completed (green)
- First report generated from live data
- First complete study lifecycle on platform
- User satisfaction survey completed (CSAT)

### Success Criteria
- 7-day health: all workflows operational, no major issues
- 30-day health: regular usage established, data flowing
- Feature adoption: at least 80% of purchased features in use
- User confidence: average CSAT > 4.0/5
- Business value: first evidence of time saved or quality improved

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Customer Project Manager | High | Weekly health checks |
| All End Users | Medium | In-app feedback, surveys |
| Executive Sponsor | Medium | 30-day business value review |
| Merline CSM | High | Proactive outreach |

### Risk Factors
- Post-launch bugs erode confidence
- Users revert to old tools
- Key trained user leaves organization
- Data quality issues reduce trust in platform
- Expected AI features not yet available (deferred to Phase 2)

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| CSM (intensive) | CSM (periodic) | Health score green for 30 days |

### Timeline
- 30-90 days post go-live

---

## Stage 9: Expansion

### Customer Activities
- Adopt additional features and modules (AI, reporting, knowledge management)
- Expand to additional projects, studies, or teams
- Invite additional users (more researchers, supervisors, enumerators)
- Expand to additional country offices or geographies
- Integrate with additional systems (DHIS2, Power BI, etc.)
- Upgrade tier (Professional → Enterprise) or add user packs
- Train new teams using existing train-the-trainer capability

### Milestones
- Additional modules activated
- User count increased by > 25%
- Additional projects/studies created
- Expansion order placed
- Cross-team or cross-country deployment

### Success Criteria
- Expansion revenue > 20% of initial contract value in Year 1
- Users in new teams reach adoption benchmarks within 30 days
- New modules adopted and used regularly
- Customer references expansion as positive experience

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Customer Project Manager | High | Expansion planning |
| Executive Sponsor | High | ROI review, expansion approval |
| New Team Leads | High | Onboarding (re-use existing training) |
| IT/Admin | Medium | New user provisioning |

### Risk Factors
- New teams resist platform (see it as "imposed")
- Existing champion burned out from first deployment
- New module complexity (e.g., AI) requires additional training
- Cross-country expansion encounters data residency issues
- Budget constraints limit expansion scope

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| CSM | Sales (expansion order) | Customer expresses expansion need |
| Sales | Implementation (if new teams) | Expansion order confirmed |

### Timeline
- 3-12 months post go-live, recurring

---

## Stage 10: Renewal

### Customer Activities
- Business value assessment (time saved, quality improved, reports generated)
- Quarterly business review with Merline CSM
- Success story documentation
- Renewal proposal review
- Contract renewal or upgrade decision
- Executive sponsor alignment on continued investment

### Milestones
- Quarterly business review completed (QBR deck delivered)
- Renewal proposal submitted 60+ days before expiry
- Executive sponsor endorses renewal
- Contract renewed (same tier or upgraded)

### Success Criteria
- Customer demonstrates measurable value from platform
- Executive sponsor actively champions renewal
- NPS score > 40 (promoter)
- Usage metrics green across all health dimensions
- No unresolved escalations

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Executive Sponsor | Very High | QBR, ROI summary, renewal conversation |
| Customer Project Manager | High | Value documentation, stakeholder alignment |
| End Users | Low-Medium | Adoption data, satisfaction surveys |
| Procurement/Legal | Medium | Renewal terms |
| Merline CSM | Very High | Renewal orchestration |

### Risk Factors
- Customer cannot demonstrate ROI (no baseline measurement)
- Executive sponsor changes; new sponsor not bought in
- Budget cuts affect renewal timeline or tier
- Competitor makes aggressive offer at renewal
- Customer's program cycle ends; no new funding secured

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| CSM | Sales (renewal) | 90 days before expiry |
| CSM | Customer Marketing | Customer willing to be a reference |

### Timeline
- Renewal decision: 30-90 days before contract end
- Annual renewal cycle

---

## Stage 11: Advocacy

### Customer Activities
- Provides reference for prospective customers
- Participates in case study or video testimonial
- Speaks at Merline conference or user group event
- Contributes to community forum (best practices, templates)
- Provides product feedback through advisory board
- Mentors new customers in train-the-trainer capacity
- Renews with multi-year commitment

### Milestones
- Reference call completed for a prospect
- Case study published
- Conference speaking engagement
- Customer advisory board participation
- Multi-year contract signed

### Success Criteria
- Customer actively promotes Merline without prompting
- Customer contributes to community (forum posts, templates, best practices)
- Customer NPS > 50 (true promoter)
- Customer has expanded beyond initial deployment
- Customer multi-year commitment

### Stakeholder Engagement
| Stakeholder | Engagement Level | Method |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| Champion (M&E Director) | Very High | Advocacy activities |
| Executive Sponsor | High | Multi-year commitment, conference presence |
| Power Users | Medium | Community contributions |
| Merline Marketing | High | Case study production |

### Risk Factors
- Customer's champion leaves organization
- Product direction diverges from customer's needs
- Competitive product offers significant improvement
- Customer's organizational priorities shift away from MERL

### Team Handoffs
| From | To | Trigger |
|------|----|---------|
| CSM | Marketing / Community | Customer agrees to be advocate |
| CSM | Product | Customer advisory board feedback loop |

### Timeline
- 12+ months post go-live, ongoing

## Handoff Matrix

| Handoff | Trigger | Sender | Receiver | Key Artifacts |
|---------|---------|--------|----------|---------------|
| Marketing → Sales | Lead qualified (BANT) | Marketing | Sales | Lead profile, engagement history |
| Sales → CS | Contract signed | Sales | Customer Success Manager | Contract, implementation scope, stakeholder map |
| CS → Implementation | Kickoff completed | CSM | Implementation Lead | Implementation plan, success criteria |
| Implementation → Training | Setup complete | Implementation Lead | Training Lead | System configuration, user list, roles |
| Training → CS | Training completion | Training Lead | CSM | Training completion report, certification records |
| CS → Support | Go-live confirmed | CSM | Support Team | Customer profile, known issues, escalation contacts |
| CS → Sales (renewal) | 90 days pre-expiry | CSM | Sales | Renewal recommendation, adoption data, expansion opportunities |
| CS → Marketing | Customer agrees to advocacy | CSM | Marketing | Customer consent, success metrics, story highlights |

## Cross-Stage Risk Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Stages Affected | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|----------------|------------|
| Champion leaves organization | Medium | Critical | Evaluation → Advocacy | Multi-stakeholder engagement, never single-thread champion |
| Data quality migration issues | Medium | High | Implementation → Go-Live | Data audit before migration, staged data migration plan |
| User resistance to new tool | High | Medium | Implementation → Optimization | Change management plan, early user involvement, champion network |
| Underestimating training needs | High | Medium | Training → Go-Live | Skill assessment before training, extended practice sessions |
| Connectivity issues in field | Medium | High | Go-Live → Optimization | Offline-first design validation, field pre-test |
| Budget cuts | Medium | High | Procurement → Renewal | Flexible pricing, annual vs monthly options, value demonstration |
| Competitor threat | Medium | Medium | Evaluation → Renewal | Continuous value demonstration, product road map alignment |
