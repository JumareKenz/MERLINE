# Unknowns & Open Questions

## Strategic Unknowns

1. **Target Market Size** - What is the total addressable market for a MERL platform?
2. **Competitive Landscape** - Who are the direct competitors and how are they positioned?
3. **Pricing Model** - Is this SaaS, enterprise license, open-core, or hybrid?
4. **Funding Source** - Is this venture-backed, grant-funded, bootstrapped, or internal?
5. **Go-to-Market** - How do organizations discover, evaluate, and adopt MERL software?

## Technical Unknowns

1. **Cloud Provider** - AWS, GCP, Azure, or multi-cloud?
2. **Deployment Regions** - Which geographies require local data residency?
3. **Scale Requirements** - What is the expected number of users, organizations, records?
4. **Integration Priorities** - Which third-party systems are highest priority for connectors?
5. **AI Model Strategy** - Which models will be primary, which secondary?

## Organizational Unknowns

1. **Team Composition** - Who will fill the executive agent roles?
2. **Development Velocity** - What is the expected throughput of the organization?
3. **Decision Authority** - Who has final approval for budget, scope, timeline?
4. **Governance Model** - Is there a steering committee, advisory board, or single decision-maker?

## Product Unknowns

1. **User Personas** - Detailed understanding of each user type's workflow and pain points
2. **MVP Definition** - What is the minimum set of capabilities for initial release?
3. **Geographic Focus** - Which regions/countries should be prioritized?
4. **Language Requirements** - Which languages must be supported initially?
5. **Regulatory Environment** - Which specific regulations apply (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)?

## Action

These unknowns should be systematically resolved through the Phase 0 planning process. Each unknown should be assigned to the appropriate executive for research and recommendation.
