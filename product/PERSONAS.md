# Merline User Personas

## Persona 1: Dr. Amara Osei — Researcher / Evaluator

### Role
Senior Research Scientist at an international development evaluation firm. Designs and leads mixed-methods evaluations for donor-funded programs.

### Background
- PhD in Public Health Epidemiology (10+ years experience)
- Has led 30+ evaluation projects across 15 countries
- Expert in quantitative and qualitative research design
- Published in peer-reviewed journals

### Goals
- Design methodologically rigorous studies that meet donor requirements
- Ensure data collection instruments are valid and reliable
- Supervise field teams remotely across multiple countries
- Produce credible evidence that influences policy and program decisions
- Maintain professional reputation for high-quality research

### Pain Points
- Spends 40% of project time on administrative and coordination work, not analysis
- Current toolkit: Word for protocols, Excel for indicators, KoboToolbox for surveys, Stata for analysis, PowerPoint for reports — five disconnected systems
- No easy way to link Theory of Change to indicators to questionnaire questions
- Data quality issues discovered weeks after collection, when correction is impossible
- Donors demand reports in specific formats; each report requires days of manual formatting

### Digital Literacy
High — proficient with statistical software, survey platforms, and data analysis tools. Prefers keyboard shortcuts and efficient workflows. Impatient with unnecessary clicks.

### Workflow Description
Designs study protocol → defines Theory of Change → develops indicator framework → builds questionnaire in survey tool → briefs field teams → monitors data collection → cleans and analyzes data → writes report → presents findings to donor.

### Key Needs
- Integrated workflow from research design to report
- Methodological guardrails that prevent common design errors
- AI-assisted questionnaire design based on indicators
- Real-time data quality monitoring during collection
- One-click report generation from live data

### What Success Looks Like
Can design a study, deploy instruments, monitor collection in real-time, and generate a donor-ready report — all from one platform, without leaving the research design context.

### Frustrations
Excel-based indicator tracking that must be manually reconciled with survey data. Discovering data quality issues too late. Reformatting the same content for different donor templates.

## Persona 2: Jean-Paul Mugisha — Field Enumerator

### Role
Community-based data collector for a national health survey. Conducts household interviews using mobile devices.

### Background
- Secondary education, trained in survey methodology
- 5 years experience as an enumerator
- Lives in a rural district with unreliable electricity and internet
- Works on 2-3 surveys per year for different organizations

### Goals
- Complete assigned surveys accurately and efficiently
- Earn reliable income per completed survey
- Avoid errors that lead to rejected work and lost pay
- Navigate to assigned households without getting lost
- Feel confident using the technology

### Pain Points
- App crashes or freezes during interviews
- Complex skip patterns cause confusion; must backtrack
- Unable to submit data when network is down; may lose data
- Battery dies during long field days
- GPS coordinates take too long to acquire
- Receives conflicting instructions from different supervisors

### Digital Literacy
Basic — comfortable with smartphones for messaging and social media. Intimidated by complex interfaces. Prefers large touch targets, clear labels in local language, and voice-guided workflows.

### Workflow Description
Receives assignment on phone → reads instructions → navigates to household → conducts interview → captures responses, photos, GPS → submits data when connected → receives next assignment.

### Key Needs
- Simple, large-button interface optimized for field use
- Complete offline functionality (forms, media, GPS)
- Long battery life; low-power mode
- Audio-supported surveys (listen to questions)
- Clear error messages in local language
- One-tap data submission with visual confirmation

### What Success Looks Like
Completes 15+ interviews per day without technical issues. Submits all data at end of day with one tap. Never loses data. Can see earnings and completed assignments in a simple dashboard.

### Frustrations
Apps that require internet to start. Complex screens that are hard to read in sunlight. Error messages in English. Losing data when app crashes. Being paid late because supervisor can't verify submissions.

## Persona 5: Grace Okonkwo — M&E Officer

### Role
Monitoring and Evaluation Officer for a government-run agricultural extension program. Manages the M&E system for 500+ field staff.

### Background
- Bachelor's in Statistics, Master's in Monitoring & Evaluation
- 7 years in government M&E systems
- Expert in indicator frameworks, LogFrames, and performance measurement
- Responsible for training field staff on M&E procedures

### Goals
- Maintain a high-quality M&E system that meets government standards
- Train and support field staff in data collection procedures
- Ensure timely and accurate reporting to ministry leadership
- Build organizational M&E capacity over time
- Integrate data from multiple sources (surveys, administrative data, partner reports)

### Pain Points
- Government M&E systems are paper-based or Excel-based; prone to errors
- Field staff have low digital literacy; need extensive training
- Multiple partners use different tools; data harmonization is difficult
- Indicator definitions change without communication; data comparability suffers
- Leadership demands reports urgently; no time for proper quality assurance
- No institutional memory; when staff leave, M&E knowledge leaves with them

### Digital Literacy
Moderate-High — comfortable with spreadsheets, databases, and basic statistical analysis. Can train others on digital tools. Familiar with DHIS2.

### Workflow Description
Defines indicators and targets for program → designs data collection tools → trains field staff → monitors data quality → validates and cleans data → produces monthly and quarterly reports → provides feedback to field staff on data quality → updates M&E manual and standard operating procedures.

### Key Needs
- Structured indicator library with version control
- Data quality scoring at submission time
- Training workflow for field staff (in-app guidance)
- Multi-source data integration
- Audit trail for all data changes
- Ability to freeze indicator definitions after baseline

### What Success Looks Like
Field staff submit high-quality data on time. Reports are generated automatically. Indicator definitions are version-controlled and cannot be changed without approval. Leadership receives dashboards, not raw data.

### Frustrations
Cleaning the same data quality issues every month. Training new field staff on the same procedures. Unable to enforce indicator definitions across partners. Excel crashes with large datasets.

### Background

- Senior government official
- Accountable to parliament and taxpayers for aid effectiveness

- Ensure development funds are achieving measurable results

IT Systems Manager for a large international NGO. Responsible for all technology systems across 20 country offices.

### Background
- Bachelor's in Computer Science
- 15 years in IT management for non-profit sector
- Manages 200+ servers, 50+ SaaS applications, 3,000+ users
- Responsible for data security, compliance, and system integration

### Goals
- Maintain secure, reliable, and compliant technology systems
- Reduce the number of SaaS platforms the organization uses
- Ensure data sovereignty and local hosting where required
- Integrate systems to reduce manual data transfer
- Support field staff with limited connectivity

### Pain Points
- Managing 30+ different SaaS accounts across the organization
- Data stored in multiple systems with no single source of truth
- Field staff use personal devices; security risks
- No centralized user management (onboarding/offboarding is manual)
- Difficult to ensure compliance with GDPR, data protection laws across 20 countries
- Each country office wants different tools; no standardization

### Digital Literacy
High — expert in IT infrastructure, security, and system administration. Can evaluate technical architecture. Expects APIs, SSO, and audit logs.

### Workflow Description
Manages user accounts and permissions → monitors system performance and uptime → ensures data security and compliance → manages integrations with other systems → provides technical support to field offices → evaluates new technology purchases → maintains disaster recovery plan.

### Key Needs
- SAML/SSO integration with organizational identity provider
- Role-based access control with granular permissions
- API access for integration with existing systems
- Audit logs for all user and system actions
- Data export and migration tools
- Multi-region deployment options for data sovereignty
- White-label options for organizational branding

### What Success Looks Like
Onboards a new country office in one day. Integrates Merline with the organization's existing identity system. Generates a compliance audit report in minutes. Field staff can collect data even in areas with no connectivity. One platform replaces five existing tools.

### Frustrations
Managing separate logins for every system. Explaining to field staff why they need yet another app. Security breaches from unsecured field devices. Data stored on servers in countries without adequate data protection laws. Manual user provisioning and deprovisioning.
